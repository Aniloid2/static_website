Utilizing symbols to improve attention mechanism attacks in NLP systems

This project presents a method to boost the performance of Textfooler for semantic similarity adversarial attacks on sentiment classification NLP models (specifically BERT).
It leverages:
  • The fact that new attention-based models use punctuation and other symbols in their processing.
  • Symbols that although shouldnâ€™t, have a sentiment attached to them.
  • A feature of semantic similarity encoders (E.g Universal Sentence Encoder by Google, Cer et al 2018), where the output word embedding resulting from a sentence is dependant mostly on the words, not the symbols.

Cascading these symbols on negative samples and positive samples respectively will flip the sentiment of the sentence while perturbing fewer words, therefore resulting in a higher semantic similarity score. The colour coding represents the changed words, where green is a changed word on the original sentence and red is the resulting changed word.


Future work includes scaling the method on BERT-Attack and CLARE (two other adversarial attack methods) with two other methods of adding symbols. One of which uses an optimization algorithm to find the best way to insert symbols within the sentence, the other, utilises a deep learning model.
